Kakum National Park

Location: Cape Coast, Central Region

Kakum National Park is a dense tropical rainforest in southern Ghana. The park is home to over 40 species of larger mammals including forest elephants, forest buffalo, Mona-meerkats, and civets. The birdlife is fantastic with over 250 species present.

×

Mole National Park

Location: Northern Region

Mole National Park is Ghana's largest wildlife refuge. The park is located in northwest Ghana on grassland savanna and riparian ecosystems. Its animals include elephants, warthogs, antelopes, and over 300 bird species. Visitors can take safari walks and drives to view wildlife.

Boti Falls

Location: Eastern Region

Boti Falls is a twin waterfall located in the Manya Krobo District. During the rainy season, the two falls merge into one massive fall. The falls are surrounded by lush vegetation and rock formations. Visitors can swim in the pool at the base of the falls.

×

Wli Waterfalls

Location: Volta Region

Wli Waterfalls is the highest waterfall in West Africa, with a drop of about 80 meters. The falls are located in the Agumatsa Wildlife Sanctuary, home to fruit bats and various bird species. The hike to the falls takes you through beautiful rainforest scenery.

×

Aburi Botanical Gardens

Location: Eastern Region

The Aburi Botanical Gardens were established in 1890 as a place of research into tropical plants and as a sanatorium for colonial officials. The gardens feature a stunning collection of tropical plants, trees and flowers, and offer panoramic views of Accra and the surrounding area.
